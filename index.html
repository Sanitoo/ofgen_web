Africa-Focused Energy Development

OFGEN is an Africa-focused energy development and infrastructure company delivering resilient, bankable, and future-ready power solutions across the continent.

We operate at the intersection of project development, infrastructure investment (financing), and Engineering, Procurement & Construction (EPC), enabling us to take projects from concept through financing, delivery, and long-term operation.

Our Vision

To be a leading African energy infrastructure platform that accelerates the transition to reliable, affordable, and sustainable power.

Our Footprint

OFGEN operates across East, West, and Southern Africa, with a strong presence in Kenya, Uganda, Tanzania, Ghana, Nigeria, and select Southern Africa markets. Our regional footprint is supported by strong local partnerships and deep regulatory understanding.

Strategy

Our core focus is on clean energy and grid infrastructure that supports economic growth, energy security, and decarbonization. We work closely with governments, utilities, C&I clients, and DFIs.

Capabilities

Concept to Operation

Developer

As a project developer, OFGEN originates, structures, and de-risks energy and grid infrastructure projects from early-stage concept to financial close.

  • Site identification and feasibility studies
  • Grid and system impact assessments
  • Regulatory approvals and permitting
  • Commercial structuring (PPAs, leases, wheeling)
  • Environmental and social compliance

Infrastructure Investor

OFGEN acts as an infrastructure investor by deploying capital directly and by structuring projects to attract third-party financing from commercial banks and DFIs.

  • Equity investment and co-development capital
  • Project finance structuring
  • Blended finance and concessional funding
  • Public-Private Partnerships (PPPs)
  • Long-term asset ownership

EPC Delivery

OFGEN provides end-to-end Engineering, Procurement & Construction services, ensuring projects are delivered on time, within budget, and to international quality standards.

  • Rooftop and ground-mounted solar PV systems
  • Captive power and behind-the-meter solutions
  • Solar for telecom towers and data infrastructure
  • Hybrid systems with storage and grid integration
  • Battery Energy Storage Systems (BESS)
  • High-voltage transmission and distribution powerlines
  • Primary and secondary substations
